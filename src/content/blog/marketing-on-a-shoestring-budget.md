The marketing technology landscape is flooded with tools that promise the world but cost a small fortune. If you're an indie hacker, solo founder, or small team, you can't afford most of them.

The good news? You don't need to.

Here are the genuinely free tools we use and recommend — no hidden "upgrade to pro" walls, no credit card required.

## 1. GitHub SEO Analyzer (That's Us)

Start with your own repos. Our [SEO Analyzer](/seo) checks any public GitHub repository for search readiness, gives a score, and provides a checklist of actionable fixes.

It's completely free, no signup required, and runs entirely in your browser.

## 2. Google Search Console

This is non-negotiable. Google Search Console tells you exactly how your site performs in search results — which queries bring traffic, which pages rank, and where you have issues.

It's free. Set it up today.

## 3. Google Analytics

Despite the competition, GA4 is still the most comprehensive free analytics platform. Track page views, user behavior, and conversion paths without spending a dime.

Pro tip: Filter out your own IP address to avoid skewed data.

## 4. Canva (Free Tier)

You don't need Photoshop to create decent marketing visuals. Canva's free tier has thousands of templates for social media posts, blog headers, presentations, and more.

## 5. MailerLite (Free up to 1,000 subscribers)

Email marketing doesn't have to be expensive. MailerLite's free plan covers up to 1,000 subscribers with all the core features: automation, landing pages, and analytics.

## 6. AnswerThePublic (Free Limited)

Get content ideas based on real search queries. Enter a keyword and see what people are actually asking about. Great for blog post ideas and FAQ sections.

## The Strategy: Stack Free Tools Strategically

The real power comes from combining these tools:

1. Use AnswerThePublic to find content ideas
2. Write the content and optimize with basic SEO principles
3. Use our SEO Analyzer to check your GitHub repos
4. Track everything with Google Search Console
5. Build an email list with MailerLite
6. Promote with visuals from Canva

That's a complete marketing stack for exactly $0.

## Bottom Line

Free tools are not inferior tools. Many are genuinely excellent products with generous free tiers. The key is knowing which ones to use and how to combine them effectively.

Start with the tools that solve your most urgent problem. For most developers, that's getting their repos and content found — which is exactly where our SEO Analyzer comes in.
